Masái Mara Conservation Area

It is a national reserve located in the southwest of Kenya, in the Narok County. It is part of the Serengeti region and is, in fact, an extension of the Serengeti National Park. It is named after the Maasai tribe that inhabits the area and the Mara River that crosses it. It is famous for its exceptional wildlife.

Covering an area of 1510 km² in the Great Rift Valley region, its surface was reduced from the 1672 km² it had in 1984. It comprises three sections: the Mara Triangle, Musiara (where Musiara Swamp is located), and Sekenani.

The majority of the territory is savannah dotted with distinctive acacias. Wildlife tends to concentrate in the western part of the valley, where access to water is easier. The eastern border of the park is about 224 km from Nairobi.

The Masai Mara is renowned for its lion population and for being the home of threatened species such as the black rhinoceros, the hippopotamus found in the Mara and Talek rivers, and the cheetah, with a significant reduction in the number of the latter. Another carnivore that can be found in large numbers is the spotted hyena.

The main inhabitants of this region are members of the Maasai community, an ancient people that has preserved its customs and traditions in the midst of the African savannah.
