Ngorongoro Conservation Area

The Ngorongoro Conservation Area is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located 180 km west of Arusha in northern Tanzania and southeast of Serengeti National Park. It covers approximately 3,200 square miles (8,300 square kilometers) and extends over the eastern part of the East African Rift Valley.

The Ngorongoro Area encompasses a variety of habitats and landscapes, including grassy plains, wooded savannahs, forests, mountains, volcanic craters, lakes, rivers, and swamps. The Ngorongoro Crater is situated within the area, where an immense concentration of wildlife coexists in its 20 km diameter.

The Ngorongoro Conservation Area is inhabited by more than 40,000 Maasai pastoralists, whose livestock share the land with wildlife. This competition for water and pasture sometimes led to conflicts in the past, especially during the dry season. However, such conflicts are rare nowadays.

The landscape of the Ngorongoro Area is dominated by the Rift Valley and volcanoes. The two main areas of the Rift Valley are north of Lake Eyasi and east of Lakes Manyara and Natron.
